# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be professional, be kind, argue about ideas.

## Expected behavior

- Critique designs, code, and arguments — never people.
- Assume good faith, especially across languages and cultures; many participants (including the maintainer) are not native English speakers.
- Disagreement is welcome and useful. Express it with reasons, accept losing an argument gracefully, and let decided things stay decided (or reopen them with new arguments, through the proper channel).
- Help newcomers. Every expert's first issue was once naive.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Insults, harassment, or discrimination of any kind, in any project space (issues, PRs, discussions, or elsewhere when representing the project).
- Sustained disruption: derailing threads, re-litigating settled decisions without new arguments, aggressive pile-ons.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.

## Enforcement

Report conduct issues privately to **conduct@ldlabs.dev**. Reports are handled confidentially by the maintainers.

Consequences scale with severity and repetition: a private warning, then a public warning, then a temporary ban, then a permanent ban. Egregious behavior (threats, harassment, doxxing) skips the ladder.

If the person you need to report *is* a maintainer, say so in your report; it will be handled with that conflict of interest on the table, and once the project has multiple maintainers (see GOVERNANCE.md), by an uninvolved one.
